How we ship a new county.

The turn-on for LA County took six weeks. Orange County should take three. Here's the actual playbook, written down so the next one goes faster — and so you can see what's happening under the hood.

1. Harvest zip requests

Every region starts with the in-app Request my area button. When a zip crosses 50 requests, it goes on the board. LA County was requested constantly for three months before we formally shipped it, which is why the soft launch was so fast — we already had the signal.

2. Audit the local chain mix

Different counties, different stores. Ventura is Aldi / TJ's / Ralphs / Vons / Sprouts. LA adds Walmart, Smart & Final, Northgate, Ranch 99, Erewhon. We audit which chains hit 60%+ of local grocery spend, then prioritize.

3. Wire the feeds

Two flavors of feed: national chains (Kroger-owned Ralphs, Albertsons-owned Vons) are a config change — same feed, different store IDs. Independents and regional chains need a light custom scraper + human verification on 1% of SKUs per week. The second category is where all the engineering time goes.

4. Run the "human check"

Before a region goes live, we physically walk five stores and spot-check 80 items per store against what the app shows. If 97%+ match shelf, we turn it on. LA County ran at 98.4% on launch day.

5. Soft-launch to requesters

The people who tapped Request my area get a push notification when their region goes live — before we say a word publicly. They test, they report bugs, we fix for a week.

6. Say it out loud

Then, and only then, the city gets a marketing push. LA County went public 8 days after soft launch. Support tickets stayed flat. Launch-week savings averaged $97 per user.

What we're changing for Orange County

The biggest lesson from LA was that Step 3 (feeds) is the bottleneck and it parallelizes badly with one person. For Orange County we're pre-wiring the regional-chain scrapers in advance so Step 3 runs alongside Steps 1 and 2 instead of after them. Target turnaround: three weeks instead of six.

If you're in a zip that isn't live yet: open the app, tap Request my area. That's how your city gets prioritized.
