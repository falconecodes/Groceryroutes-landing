Boilerplate

Grocery Routes is an iOS app that compares every item on a shopper's grocery list across the stores they shop at, and returns the single cheapest multi-store trip. Launched in January 2026 and built independently out of Ventura County, California. Typical users save $100+ per 70-item list. Free, with nationwide expansion targeted for early 2027.

On the record.

"I grew up here. I know what it’s like to watch your cart total climb and just hope for the best at checkout. I built GroceryRoutes because nobody should have to guess which store is cheaper — especially when a few miles can mean $20 back in your pocket.”

— Founder, Grocery Routes

"I’m not a big company. I’m one person from Ventura County who wanted to make something useful for the people around me. If this app saves a family in Oxnard or Camarillo $30 a week, that’s the whole point.”

— Founder, Grocery Routes

"I built this without a team, without funding, and without a background in tech. I built it because I needed it, and I figured if I needed it, my neighbors probably did too."
